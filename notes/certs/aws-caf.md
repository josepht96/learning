# AWS Cloud Adoption Framework (CAF)

## Overview
The AWS Cloud Adoption Framework provides comprehensive guidance to help organizations design and implement a cloud adoption strategy. It organizes guidance into six perspectives that span people, process, and technology.

## Six Perspectives

### 1. Business Perspective
**Focus:** Ensure cloud investments accelerate business outcomes

**Key Stakeholders:**
- Business managers
- Finance managers
- Budget owners
- Strategy stakeholders

**Core Capabilities:**
- Strategy management
- Portfolio management
- Innovation management
- Product management
- Strategic partnership
- Data monetization
- Business insight

**Goals:**
- Create strong business case for cloud adoption
- Align cloud strategy with business goals
- Demonstrate value and ROI
- Prioritize cloud initiatives based on business impact

---

### 2. People Perspective
**Focus:** Bridge between technology and business by accelerating cultural change

**Key Stakeholders:**
- HR
- People managers
- Staffing leads

**Core Capabilities:**
- Culture evolution
- Transformational leadership
- Cloud fluency
- Workforce transformation
- Change acceleration
- Organizational design
- Organizational alignment

**Goals:**
- Evaluate organizational structures and roles
- Update staff skills and processes
- Create cloud-fluent workforce
- Manage change effectively

---

### 3. Governance Perspective
**Focus:** Maximize organizational benefits while minimizing transformation-related risks

**Key Stakeholders:**
- CIO
- Program managers
- Enterprise architects
- Business analysts
- Portfolio managers

**Core Capabilities:**
- Program and project management
- Benefits management
- Risk management
- Cloud financial management
- Application portfolio management
- Data governance
- Data curatorship

**Goals:**
- Understand how to update staff skills and processes
- Measure cloud ROI
- Manage and optimize costs
- Implement proper governance models

---

### 4. Platform Perspective
**Focus:** Build enterprise-grade, scalable, hybrid cloud platform

**Key Stakeholders:**
- CTO
- IT managers
- Solutions architects

**Core Capabilities:**
- Platform architecture
- Platform engineering
- Data architecture
- Data engineering
- Provisioning and orchestration
- Modern application development
- Continuous integration/continuous delivery (CI/CD)

**Goals:**
- Design, implement, and optimize cloud architecture
- Build scalable and resilient infrastructure
- Implement modern application architectures
- Automate infrastructure provisioning

---

### 5. Security Perspective
**Focus:** Achieve confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data and cloud workloads

**Key Stakeholders:**
- CISO
- IT security managers
- IT security analysts

**Core Capabilities:**
- Security governance
- Security assurance
- Identity and access management
- Threat detection
- Vulnerability management
- Infrastructure protection
- Data protection
- Application security
- Incident response

**Goals:**
- Structure selection and implementation of security controls
- Meet compliance and security objectives
- Implement defense-in-depth approach
- Ensure secure operations

---

### 6. Operations Perspective
**Focus:** Ensure cloud services are delivered at agreed-upon service levels

**Key Stakeholders:**
- IT operations managers
- IT support managers

**Core Capabilities:**
- Observability
- Event management (AIOps)
- Incident and problem management
- Change and release management
- Performance and capacity management
- Configuration management
- Patch management
- Availability and continuity management
- Application management

**Goals:**
- Define how business is conducted in the cloud
- Monitor and optimize cloud operations
- Ensure service levels are met
- Implement operational best practices

---

## Cloud Transformation Journey

### Four Phases

1. **Envision**
   - Demonstrate how cloud will accelerate business outcomes
   - Build initial business case
   - Identify transformation opportunities

2. **Align**
   - Identify capability gaps across perspectives
   - Create action plans to close gaps
   - Align stakeholders

3. **Launch**
   - Deliver pilot initiatives in production
   - Demonstrate incremental business value
   - Build credibility and experience

4. **Scale**
   - Expand production pilots
   - Realize desired business outcomes
   - Scale across the organization

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Holistic Approach:** CAF addresses people, process, and technology
- **Not Just Technical:** Emphasizes business alignment and cultural change
- **Iterative:** Cloud adoption is a journey, not a one-time project
- **Customizable:** Organizations adapt the framework to their specific needs
- **Cross-functional:** Requires collaboration across all perspectives

---

## Common Exam Topics

- Know the six perspectives and their focus areas
- Understand which stakeholders align with which perspectives
- Recognize the four transformation phases
- Understand that CAF is about organizational transformation, not just technology
